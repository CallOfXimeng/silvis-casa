## <font color="#8246af"> 1st personal pronouns  </font>

| Nominative                                                           | ego  | I         | nos              | we      |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---- | --------- | ---------------- | ------- |
| <span style="color: #00c389; font-weight: bold;"> Accusative </span> | me   | me        | nos              | us      |
| <span style="color: #00c389; font-weight: bold;">Genitive </span>    | mei  | of me     | nostri / nostrum | of us   |
| <span style="color: #00c389; font-weight: bold;"> Dative </span>     | mihi | to me     | nobis            | to us   |
| <span style="color: #00c389; font-weight: bold;"> Ablative </span>   | me   | (from) me | nobis            | from us |

**1st Person Singular: "I" (Ego, Me, Mei, Mihi, Me)**
**Nominative** (Subject): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;"> Ego in horto sum. </font> (I am in the garden.)
**Accusative** (Object): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;"> Tu me vides. </span> (You see me.)
**Genitive** (Objective/Partitive): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;"> Memoria mei est bona. </span> (The memory of me is good. (Not *"my memory,"* but the act of remembering me)).
**Dative** (Indirect Object - "To/For"): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;"> Amicus mihi cibum dat. </span> (The friend gives food to me. Dat = gives).
**Ablative** (Prepositions): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;"> Tu mecum (me + cum) ambulas. </span> (You walk with me.)

**1st Person Plural: "We" (Nos, Nostri/Nostrum, Nobis, Nos, Nobis)**
**Nominative** (Subject): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;"> Nos aquam portamus.</span>(We carry the water.)
**Accusative** (Object): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;">  Regina nos vocat. </span> (The queen calls us.)
**Genitive** (Partitive): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;">  Unus nostrum adest. </span>(One of us is present.)
**Dative** (Indirect Object - "To/For"): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;">  Dominus nobis pecuniam dat.</span>(The master gives money to us.)
**Ablative** (Prepositions): <span style="color: #407ec9; font-weight: bold;">  Dominus nobiscum (nobis + cum) est. </span>(The Lord is with us.)


<span style="background-color: #d7c6e6; border: 1px solid #8246af; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px; font-weight: bold;"> In Latin, verbs like "give," "show," or "tell" usually have two objects:</span>
```
    The thing being given (Accusative - ends in -m).
    The person receiving it (Dative - Mihi / Nobis).
```
**Example**: Regina *mihi* (to me) pecuniam (money) *dat* (gives).

## <font color="#8246af"> 1st Possessive pronouns  </font>
These possessive pronouns **decline** like adjectives. They indicate possession (to whom something belongs).

| Latin                   | Means in English                 |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| meus, mea, meum         | my                               |
| tuus, tua, tuum         | your, yours (one person)         |
| noster, nostra, nostrum | our, ours                        |
| vester, vestra, vestrum | your, yours (two or more people) |
